Moral Psychology
Quantitative Methods

The Effect of Manipulated Attention in Cumulative Moral Learning from Consequences

Research Assistant | Supervisor: Dr. Falk Lieder, UCLA | April 2025 – Present

Examines how experimentally manipulated attentional allocation shapes cumulative moral learning from action outcomes across repeated moral decisions.

Kinship Mindfulness Project

Research Assistant | Advisor: Dr. Xiaotian Wang, CUHK-Shenzhen | Dec 2025 – Present

Develops a series of kinship mindfulness and compare its effect to traditional meditation mindfulness. We aim to author a book chapter regarding this new mindfulness tool.

Cross-Temporal Trends in Weight Bias Internalization Across Two Decades: A Meta-analysis

Project leader | Advisor: Dr. Jinbo He, XJTLU | Dec 2025 – Present

A meta-analytical project to examine the trend in weight bias internalization / weight self-stigma among females and males.

Maladaptive Metacognitive Beliefs Hinder Chinese Adolescents’ Academic Performance Through Heightened Anxiety

Project Leader | Advisor: Dr. Chun Chen, CUHK-Shenzhen | Nov 2024 – July 2025

Investigates how maladaptive metacognitive beliefs impair academic performance via anxiety, with emotion dysregulation amplifying this indirect effect among Chinese adolescents.

Positive Emotional Valence of Video Amplifies the Mood-Enhancing Effect of Screen Use While Eating

Project Leader | Advisor: Dr. Jinbo He, XJTLU | April 2024 – Present

Tests whether positive emotional video content enhances mood and eating experiences during screen use in a controlled laboratory setting.

Longitudinal Moderation of Social Emotional Competence on the Effect of Achievement Goals

Project Leader | Advisor: Dr. Chun Chen, CUHK-Shenzhen | April 2025 – Present

Explores how social emotional competence longitudinally moderates the effect of achievement goals on subsequent academic performance.

The Effect of Positive Psychology Intervention Among Chinese Students

Data Analyst | Advisor: Dr. Chun Chen, CUHK-Shenzhen | July 2024 – Present

Evaluates the longitudinal effectiveness of a school-based culturall-adapted positive psychology intervention on Chinese students’ socio-emotional, behavioral, and well-being outcomes.

Dynamic SEM and Temporal Network Analysis of Psychological Dynamics in Chinese School Teachers

Data Analyst | Advisor: Dr. Shi Yu, CUHK-Shenzhen | April 2025 – Present

Use dynamic structural equation model (DSEM) to model cross-level moderation to understand daily psychological adaptation among Chinese school teachers.
